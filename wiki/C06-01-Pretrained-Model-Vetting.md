# C6.1 Pretrained Model Vetting & Origin Integrity

> **Parent:** [C06: Supply Chain Security](C06-Supply-Chain.md) | **Requirements:** 5 | **IDs:** 6.1.1–6.1.5

## Purpose

Pretrained models downloaded from public hubs (Hugging Face, Model Zoo, Civitai, etc.) represent one of the highest-risk supply-chain entry points in AI systems. Unlike traditional software dependencies, model weight files can encode arbitrary executable code (via pickle deserialization), contain hidden backdoor triggers, or carry undisclosed licensing or data-provenance obligations. This section ensures every third-party model is authenticated, scanned, and reviewed before it enters the organization's pipeline.

## Requirements

| # | Requirement | Level | Role | Threat Mitigated | Verification Approach | Gaps / Notes |
|---|-------------|:-----:|:----:|-----------------|----------------------|--------------|
| **6.1.1** | **Verify that** every third-party model artifact includes a signed origin-and-integrity record identifying its source, version, and integrity checksum. | 1 | D/V | **Tampered or substituted model weights.** An attacker replaces a legitimate model file on a public hub with a trojaned version. Without checksums, the swap is invisible. (MITRE ATLAS AML.T0010.002). The OWASP LLM Top 10 2025 elevated Supply Chain from #5 to **LLM03**, reflecting the growing severity of model-origin attacks. The EU AI Act GPAI obligations (in force August 2025) require providers to maintain technical documentation and share provenance information with downstream users. | Check that every imported model has a corresponding manifest containing: publisher identity, version tag, and SHA-256/SHA-512 hash. Verify the signature chain back to the publisher's public key. Tools: Sigstore/cosign for model signing, Hugging Face commit hashes, in-toto attestations. Cross-reference against CycloneDX ML-BOM or SPDX AI profile for standardized provenance metadata. | Model signing is still immature. Hugging Face added GPG commit signing in 2023, but most model publishers do not sign. Organizations may need to self-sign upon first import and treat the internal hash as the trust anchor. NIST AI RMF "Map" function now explicitly calls for AI-BOMs, acknowledging traditional SBOMs do not capture model-specific risks. EU AI Act enforcement powers take effect August 2026, with fines for non-compliance. |
| **6.1.2** | **Verify that** models are scanned for malicious layers or Trojan triggers using automated tools before import. | 1 | D/V | **Pickle deserialization RCE and embedded malware.** PyTorch `.pt`/`.bin` files use Python pickle, which can execute arbitrary code on load. Backdoored models may also contain hidden trigger patterns that activate on specific inputs. (CVE-2024-3568 in Hugging Face Transformers, JFrog 2024 Hugging Face malware findings). **2025 incidents:** (1) *nullifAI attack* (Feb 2025) — ReversingLabs discovered malicious models on Hugging Face using broken/corrupt pickle streams and 7z compression to evade Picklescan; payloads were reverse shells connecting to hardcoded IPs. Hugging Face removed them within 24 hours. (2) *MalHug detection pipeline* found 91 malicious models and 9 malicious dataset-loading scripts across 705K+ models on Hugging Face. (3) JFrog disclosed three critical PickleScan zero-days (CVE-2025-10155, CVE-2025-10156, CVE-2025-10157, all CVSS 9.3) exploiting divergence between how PickleScan parses and PyTorch loads files — patched in PickleScan 0.0.31 (Sep 2025). | Run **multiple** scanning tools — no single scanner is sufficient given demonstrated bypass rates. Primary tools: **Protect AI ModelScan** (supports H5, Pickle, SavedModel; commercial Guardian extends to 35+ formats), **Promptfoo ModelAudit** (42+ formats, allowlist-first architecture, SARIF output for CI/CD, detected 16 issues vs. ModelScan's 3 in head-to-head testing), **Trail of Bits Fickling** (pickle decompilation + allowlist scanner). Prefer SafeTensors/ONNX format over pickle-based formats. For Trojan detection, run Neural Cleanse, ABS, or MNTD scans on candidate models. | Trojan-trigger scanning (Neural Cleanse, etc.) is computationally expensive and has known false-negative rates. **Pickle scanning alone is insufficient:** the nullifAI attack and JFrog zero-days proved that blocklist-based scanners can be evaded. Defense-in-depth requires: (1) multiple scanners with different detection architectures, (2) format restrictions (SafeTensors-only policy where feasible), and (3) sandboxed model loading. Enforcing SafeTensors as the only accepted format is the strongest control but limits model sources. The academic paper "The Art of Hide and Seek" (2025) demonstrates further stealthy pickle poisoning techniques. |
| **6.1.3** | **Verify that** model licenses, export-control tags, and data-origin statements are recorded in an AI BOM entry. | 2 | V | **Legal and compliance risk from undisclosed training data or license terms.** Models trained on copyrighted, GDPR-covered, or export-controlled data can expose the organization to lawsuits or regulatory action. The EU AI Act GPAI obligations (effective August 2025) require providers to publish sufficiently detailed summaries of training-data content, comply with EU copyright law, and share documentation with regulators and downstream deployers. Non-compliance fines apply from August 2026. | Audit the AI BOM for each model artifact. Confirm it includes: SPDX license identifier, data-origin summary (e.g., "trained on Common Crawl + proprietary corpus"), and export-control classification (EAR/ITAR if applicable). Cross-reference against the model card if one exists. Use CycloneDX ML-BOM or SPDX AI profile as the BOM format. Tools like Promptfoo ModelAudit can generate SBOMs and extract license information as part of scanning. | Many open-weight models lack clear data-origin disclosures. Organizations may need to perform independent provenance research. EU AI Act GPAI obligations are now enforceable — providers of models placed on market before August 2025 must comply by August 2027. NIST AI RMF explicitly calls for AI-BOMs. The systemic-risk tier (models exceeding 10^25 FLOP) carries additional safety and security obligations. |
| **6.1.4** | **Verify that** high-risk models (e.g., publicly uploaded weights, unverified creators) remain quarantined until human review and sign-off. | 2 | D/V | **Deployment of unvetted or backdoored models.** Automated pipelines may pull and deploy models without human judgment, allowing compromised artifacts to reach production. The MalHug study found 91 malicious models across 705K+ on Hugging Face — demonstrating that public hubs contain real threats at scale, not just theoretical risks. The Rapid7 "From .pth to p0wned" analysis (2025) documented end-to-end exploitation chains from model download to remote access trojan deployment. | Confirm that a quarantine zone (separate registry, staging namespace, or approval gate) exists. Verify that models from unverified publishers cannot progress past the quarantine stage without a documented human approval record (e.g., Jira ticket, signed review form). Run automated scanning (ModelScan, ModelAudit, Fickling) during quarantine. Integrate Palo Alto AI Runtime Security or Black Duck AI model scanning (released Oct 2025) for enterprise-grade quarantine workflows. | Defining "high-risk" criteria requires organizational policy. Suggested triggers: unknown publisher, no model card, pickle format, model size anomalies, flagged by automated scanning, or namespace squatting indicators. Commercial tools like Protect AI Guardian and Black Duck now offer managed quarantine-and-scan pipelines. |
| **6.1.5** | **Verify that** transfer-learning fine-tunes pass adversarial evaluation to detect hidden behaviors. | 3 | D | **Sleeper-agent attacks and backdoor persistence through fine-tuning.** Research (Anthropic "Sleeper Agents" 2024, BadNets, TrojAI) shows that backdoors in base models can survive fine-tuning. An attacker contributes a poisoned base model; the victim fine-tunes it, unknowingly inheriting the backdoor. The "Models Are Codes" study (2024) systematically measured malicious code poisoning across pre-trained model hubs, confirming that poisoned models are a real and growing phenomenon, not just a lab demonstration. | Run adversarial test suites against fine-tuned models, including: trigger-pattern sweeps (randomized input perturbations), activation-clustering analysis, and behavioral probes for known attack patterns. Compare fine-tuned model behavior against a clean baseline on a held-out adversarial evaluation set. The PickleBall framework (2025) proposes secure deserialization of pickle-based ML models as a complementary control. | This is computationally expensive and currently lacks standardized tooling. The TrojAI program (IARPA/NIST) is developing benchmarks. Most organizations will need to build custom adversarial evaluation pipelines. Level 3 is appropriate given the tooling gap. EU AI Act systemic-risk GPAI models (>10^25 FLOP) face additional safety evaluation requirements that may drive tooling maturity in this area. |

## Open Research Questions

- What is the false-negative rate of current Trojan-detection tools (Neural Cleanse, ABS, MNTD) against state-of-the-art backdoor attacks? The "Art of Hide and Seek" (2025) paper suggests attackers continue to outpace defenses.
- Can model provenance be established for models where training data and training process are not disclosed (closed-weight models accessed via API)?
- How should organizations handle models that are "fine-tune chains" (Model A fine-tuned from Model B fine-tuned from Model C) where provenance may be lost at each step?
- Is there a viable equivalent of Sigstore/cosign for model weight signing that the community will adopt at scale?
- **How should organizations layer multiple scanning tools given demonstrated bypasses?** The JFrog PickleScan zero-days (CVE-2025-10155/10156/10157) and the nullifAI evasion show that no single scanner is reliable. What is the right combination of blocklist (PickleScan), allowlist (ModelAudit/Fickling), and format-restriction (SafeTensors-only) controls?
- **Will EU AI Act GPAI obligations drive standardization of model provenance metadata?** The August 2025 requirements for training-data documentation and downstream transparency may create de facto standards, but enforcement details remain unclear until August 2026.
- **Can the PickleBall secure-deserialization approach scale to production workloads?** If so, it could provide a runtime defense complementing static scanning.
